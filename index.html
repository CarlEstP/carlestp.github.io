Bank case study

* Based on the guided challenge from thePower Data & Analytics.

Stack Data & Analyst:

  • Python
  • Pandas
  • Numpy
  • Matplotlib / Seaborn / Plotly

Project goals and intentions:

  • EDA: exploratory data analysis
  • Data cleaning
  • Handling missing values
  • Data visualization

Context and scenario:

Analize a portuguese bank dataset and determine whether their long term deposit product will be subscribed to or not, and draw conclusions from it.

Full project at Github

Conclusions after analyzing the dataset:

  • The campaign is not performing well, as almost 90% of the calls are not fruitful and customers are not subscribing.
  • The number of calls over time has not fluctuated much, with a uniform distribution of calls per month, as well as the distribution between subscribers and non-subscribers.
  • The longer the call, the easier it is for the customer to decide to accept.
  • After 10 contacts with the same customer during the same campaign, they are very unlikely to subscribe. However, if the contacts were made before this campaign, the results are reversed.
  • The campaign seems to be more convincing to users over 60 years old, and it reaches more married users compared to singles.
  • Mainly, subscriptions come from calls made to landlines rather than mobile phones. This could be due to calls being marked as spam or it being more difficult for people to answer calls from unknown numbers on their mobiles.
  • People who subscribed in the previous campaign have a high rate of non-subscriptions in this one.

Full project at Github

Find Your Perfect Roommate

* Practical case adapted from the proposal of the DS4B channel

Stack Data & Analyst:

  • Python
  • Pandas / Numpy
  • Matplotlib / Seaborn / Plotly
  • Scikit-learn
  • Streamlit

Project goals and intentions:

  • EDA: exploratory data analysis
  • Generation of similarity matrix
  • Visualization of results graphs

Context and scenario:

Analyze and determine which roommate is suitable from among 12,000 possible candidates based on their responses to 17 questions, to share an apartment with multiple rooms where at least one of them is already occupied.

Full project at Github

App logic description:

  • Launch the Application: Start the app using app.py, which generates the frontend with Streamlit.
  • Reformat Identifiers: Access functions.py to reformat the identifiers of the actual occupants entered in the form.
  • Find Compatible Occupants: Execute the find_compatible_occupants function using the OneHotEncoder library. Calculate the similarity matrix with a dot product, define the target range, and create a new DataFrame with Pandas.
  • Display Compatibility Data: Present the compatible occupants in a compatibility chart and display the similarity data in a table, reflecting responses to the 17 questions.

Full project at Github

Work Attendance Analysis

* Based on a project from Codebasics.io

Stack Data & Analyst:

  • Excel
  • Power Query
  • Power BI

Project goals and intentions:

  • Working preferences: home or office.
  • Presence of employees at work.
  • Percentage of overall sick leave & wellness.
  • Show visualizacions and trendings and compare KPI during time.

Context and scenario:

We received a dataset containing information about the work attendance of all employees. The company would like to analyze trends, scale, and significance during the year calendar.

Full project at Github

Conclusions after analyzing the dataset:

  • The attendance at work decreases as the summer months approach.
  • Remote work increases as the summer months approach.
  • The employee sick leave increases as summer approaches.
  • Employees prefers remote work on friday, followed by monday, and then the rest of the days.
